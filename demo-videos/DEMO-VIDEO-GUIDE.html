OsirisCare Demo Video Production Guide

Step-by-step playbook for creating AI-narrated product demo videos with avatar overlay

1. Overview — What You're Building

Each demo video has three layers composited together:

Layer 1: Screen Recording

You record your screen (mouse movements, clicks, navigation through the OsirisCare dashboard). This is the main visual content.

Layer 2: AI Voice Narration

ElevenLabs generates studio-quality voice from the script text. Sounds natural, not robotic.

Layer 3: AI Avatar (Circle Bubble)

HeyGen generates a talking-head video synced to the narration audio. FFmpeg composites it as a circular overlay in the corner of your screen recording.

Screen Recording + AI Voice + AI Avatar = Final Video
Tip: You can start with voice-only (no avatar) — the pipeline supports --voice-only mode. Add the avatar later when you're happy with the narration.

3. Production Workflow — Per Video

Repeat these steps for each of the 7 demo videos. Total time: ~30-45 min per video.

1
Review the script 5 min
Open the script in demo-videos/scripts/. Read through it. The [SCREEN: ...] lines are stage directions — they tell you what to show on screen. The plain text is what the AI voice will say.
Edit the narration if you want to customize anything.
2
Record your screen 10-15 min
Open dashboard.osiriscare.net in Safari/Chrome. Use macOS screen recording (Cmd+Shift+5) or OBS.
  • Follow the [SCREEN: ...] directions in the script
  • Move the mouse deliberately and smoothly — no rushing
  • Pause 2-3 seconds between scenes (you'll trim later)
  • Record at 1920x1080 (or your native resolution)
  • Don't worry about audio — the AI does the talking
Save to: demo-videos/recordings/01-dashboard-tour.mov
3
Generate AI voice 1-2 min
cd demo-videos
python generate.py scripts/01-dashboard-tour.md --voice-only
This extracts the narration text, sends it to ElevenLabs, and saves the audio to avatars/01-dashboard-tour-audio.mp3.
Listen to it. Re-run with edits if the pacing or pronunciation is off.
4
Generate AI avatar video 3-5 min
python generate.py scripts/01-dashboard-tour.md --avatar-only
This uploads the audio to HeyGen, generates a talking-head video with green screen background, and saves to avatars/01-dashboard-tour-avatar.mp4.
HeyGen rendering takes 2-4 min — the script polls until done.
5
Composite the final video 1-2 min
./compose.sh recordings/01-dashboard-tour.mov avatars/01-dashboard-tour-avatar.mp4
FFmpeg composites the avatar as a circle bubble (bottom-right, 200px, with drop shadow) over your screen recording, mixing the avatar voice audio with quiet screen audio.
Output: output/01-dashboard-tour-final.mp4
6
Review & adjust 5 min
Watch the output. Common tweaks:
  • Avatar too small? --size 250
  • Wrong corner? --position bottom-left
  • Screen recording too long? Trim in iMovie/QuickTime first
  • Voice too fast? Edit the script to add pauses (add "..." or shorter sentences)
Important: Match the screen recording length to the audio length. If your recording is 90 seconds but the narration is 75 seconds, the avatar will disappear early. Trim your recording or pad with a still frame.

What to show: Login → dashboard → compliance score → incidents → evidence → fleet

Key selling point: Live system, not a mockup

What to show: Evidence page → packet download → show PDF contents → blockchain anchor

Key selling point: Audit-ready evidence in one click

Video 02 — Incident Healing ~120s

What to show: Active incident → L1 auto-heal → L2 LLM → L3 escalation → evidence trail

Key selling point: Three-tier auto-healing pipeline

Director's notes:

  • BEFORE you hit record: Open a terminal to the physical appliance
  • Trigger the drift: Run systemctl stop firewall on the appliance via SSH (NixOS uses firewall, not firewalld) — this creates a firewall drift that L1 will auto-heal
  • Switch to the browser, navigate to Incidents page
  • Wait ~2-3 minutes for the next scan cycle to detect the drift
  • Record the moment the incident appears — show it go from "active" to "resolved" as L1 heals it
  • Click into the incident to show the full detail: detection timestamp, resolution method (L1 Deterministic), runbook used, evidence hash
  • For the L3 escalation portion: the existing WIN-DEPLOY-UNREACHABLE incidents in your dashboard are real L3s — click into one to show the escalation chain
  • End on the evidence trail showing the cryptographic proof of remediation

Recording strategy (two takes, stitched):

  1. Take A: Start recording. SSH in, run systemctl stop firewall. Switch to browser. Navigate to Incidents. Wait. Capture the incident appearing + auto-healing. (~3-4 min raw, trim to ~60s)
  2. Take B: Click into the healed incident for detail view. Also click into an existing L3 escalation. Show evidence trail. (~2 min raw, trim to ~60s)
  3. FFmpeg stitches both takes under continuous AI narration
Timing tip: The daemon scans every ~10 minutes. To avoid waiting on camera, trigger the drift, then start recording ~8 minutes later when the scan is about to fire. Check the appliance's last checkin time on the Sites page to estimate the next scan. Or: trigger, wait for the incident to appear, THEN start recording and scroll through the just-resolved incident as if it just happened.
Video 05 — Drift Detection ~90s

What to show: Site detail → drift config → show scan results → auto-remediation

Key selling point: Catch it before the auditor

Director's notes:

  • Show the toggle switches for all the drift checks (firewall, patching, backup, encryption, etc.) — each now shows its HIPAA control reference (e.g., §164.312(a)(1)) next to the label
  • Demo the new N/A exception feature: click the "N/A" button on Time Machine, enter "Cloud backup via BAA with EHR vendor" as the reason — shows practices can document exceptions
  • Navigate to the site detail → Compliance tab or Incidents tab filtered to drift events
  • Show the scan history — regular 10-minute intervals, each producing compliance data
  • If you already recorded #02, you have fresh drift data from the firewall stop. Reuse that same incident as the "drift detected and remediated" example
  • If you want a different drift: ssh root@192.168.88.241 then echo "PermitRootLogin yes" >> /etc/ssh/sshd_config && systemctl restart sshd — the SSH config drift check will catch this
  • Key moment: show the timeline of "drift detected → incident created → L1 auto-healed → next scan shows compliant again"
Shortcut: If you did Video #02 first, you already have a fresh heal cycle in the data. Just navigate to it from the site detail view — no need to trigger another drift.

6. Avatar Setup

Option A: Stock Avatar (quickest)

  1. Go to heygen.com → Avatars → browse stock avatars
  2. Pick a professional-looking presenter (suit/business casual)
  3. Copy the Avatar ID from the URL or API settings
  4. Paste into your .env as HEYGEN_AVATAR_ID

Option B: Instant Avatar (best — uses your face)

  1. HeyGen → Avatars → Create Instant Avatar
  2. Record a 2-minute video of yourself talking (follow their prompts)
  3. Wait ~5 min for processing
  4. Copy the Avatar ID
Tip: Instant Avatar is the most seamless result. It lip-syncs to any audio you provide and looks like a real video call. Stock avatars are good but viewers may recognize them from other HeyGen content.

7. Screen Recording Tips

  • Use Safari or Chrome at 1920x1080 (or retina equivalent)
  • Dark mode ON — the dashboard looks best in dark mode
  • Close all other tabs, hide bookmarks bar
  • Turn off macOS notifications (Focus → Do Not Disturb)
  • Move mouse smoothly — pretend you're giving a live demo
  • Hover over elements for 1-2 seconds before clicking (gives viewer time to read)
  • Pause 3 seconds at the start and end (trim margin)
  • Use Cmd+Shift+5 → "Record Selected Portion" to crop to just the browser
  • Keep recordings under 2 minutes (matching script duration)
  • No need to record audio — mute your mic
Before recording: Make sure the dashboard has real data showing (incidents, compliance scores, evidence bundles). Login to dashboard.osiriscare.net and verify the numbers look good. Empty dashboards kill the demo.
