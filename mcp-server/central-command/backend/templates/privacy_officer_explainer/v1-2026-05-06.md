# Designating Your Privacy Officer

## What you are accepting

You are designating a named individual as the **Privacy Officer** for your practice under 45 CFR §164.308(a)(2). The designated person is responsible for the development and implementation of the policies and procedures the HIPAA Privacy Rule requires of your practice as a Covered Entity.

## What this designation does

- Records who is accountable for HIPAA Privacy Rule policies and procedures at your practice.
- Names the individual who reviews monthly evidence summaries produced by OsirisCare's monitoring program.
- Provides the signature line on every Compliance Attestation Letter your practice issues to insurance carriers, board members, auditors, or counsel.

## What is published

When your practice issues a Compliance Attestation Letter, the following information from this designation appears on the letter:

- The Privacy Officer's **name**
- The Privacy Officer's **title**
- The Privacy Officer's **email address**
- The date you accepted this designation

These letters are typically forwarded by your practice to insurance carriers, board members, auditors, or attorneys. The Privacy Officer's name and title also appear on OsirisCare's public verification page (`osiriscare.io/verify/...`) so a recipient can independently confirm the letter is authentic.

If the designated person should not have their email published on customer-facing artifacts, do not designate them.

## What you are confirming

By clicking Accept, you confirm:

1. The named individual has agreed to serve as the practice's Privacy Officer under §164.308(a)(2).
2. You are authorized under your practice's governing documents (operating agreement, bylaws, partnership agreement, or equivalent) to designate the Privacy Officer on behalf of the practice.
3. You understand that the Privacy Officer's name, title, and email will appear on Compliance Attestation Letters and on OsirisCare's public verification page.

## What you can change

You can revoke this designation at any time from Settings. Revocation does NOT invalidate previously-issued attestation letters — those remain valid evidence of the designation in effect at issuance. New letters cannot be issued until a new designation is in place.

## Audit trail

This acceptance creates a cryptographically signed record (Ed25519, hash-chained, OpenTimestamps anchored) including:

- Your name, title, and email (the designee)
- The accepting user's email and IP address
- The date and time of acceptance
- The version identifier of this explainer text (`v1-2026-05-06`)
- A SHA-256 hash of this explainer text

The signed record is preserved in your practice's compliance evidence chain.
